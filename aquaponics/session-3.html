pH

For ideal function of all elements, 6-7.5 pH is optimal. (6.8ish is best)

WHEN IN DOUBT; DON'T DO ANYTHING. FILTERING IS OKAY THOUGH

Ammonium

pH is basically the amount of free-floating hydrogen ions. Ammonia picks up a hydrogen ion and converts to ammonium. Too much ammonia will kill the fish.

Diseases

Streptococcus + Aeromonas

"Strep" is the most common disease found among tilapia.

Symptoms:

  1. Lethargic,
  2. Weak,
  3. Loss of appetite,
  4. May swim erratically,
  5. Red discoloration near fins and tail,
  6. Protruding eyes,
  7. Bumps / Growths.

Solution:

  1. Reduce feeding,
  2. Cool down water,
  3. Get them out,
  4. Salty tank for an hour or two. Salt kills off disease.

Trichodynia

A parasite that infects the scales and gills of a fish.

Symptoms:

  1. Rapid breathing,
  2. Rubbing against walls.

Solution:

  1. Kill off the infected.
  2. Examine fish before / salt cleanse of fish.

Columnaris

A bacterial Disease

Symptoms:

  1. A bump on the back of the fish,
  2. Weakness,
  3. Loss of appetite,

Solution:

  1. Kill off the infected.
  2. Examine fish before / salt cleanse of fish.

Diagnosis

Fish don't sell well. Sometime it's more efficient, money-wise, to just say goodbye to the fish.

Impact of Environment on Plants

Under high temperatures, plants evaporate water, refilling from roots. If not enough water at roots, or can't refill fast enough, plant wilts and dies.

From air movement, the stem of a plant is stimulated to grow stronger as it sways.

Plants Impact pH

Certain processes in the plant increase pH, others decrease it. If intervening, be careful because it may cause drastic reverse fluctuations.
