Considerations for Aquaponics Systems - Fish

Aquaponics systems are not meant to be an ideal space for fish; they are stressful and unclean to optimize for nutrient availability for plants. Thus, resilient fish are best suited for aquaponics systems.

1. Fish Biology,

General Fishey-ness

The fish you choose to keep should match your crop. (I.E If your crop needs a colder climate, use fish that would live in cold waters.)

Male fish are aggressive during mating. Males and females can be mixed in one pool, but only below 23 degrees Celsius, otherwise they mate. Coincidentally, plants tend to prefer that temperature, too.

Gills can be used to feed, breath and maintain homeostasis.

Keeping Them Alive

You can only increase density till a certain point, after which stress and disease increases too much and any denser is counterproductive.

The most important for the fish is to filter. Food availability and temperature will vary in natural environments, so they can weather you forgetting to feed them or the temperature changing some amount, but filtering is important.

Fish have a "Photoperiod", they sense day and nighttime. From this, they know when to sleep, and when to be active. They will not only well- not be able to see food in the water if it is dark, but they also won't want to eat, because to them it's bedtime. Generally, fish area also less aggressive in the shade, so some amount of constant shading is for the best.

Efficiency

They are basically a form of bioreactor, converting fish food, to ammonia. With this though, means that and incorrect pH or temperature will slow down their output. (I.E. they will stop eating and pooping as much)

Optimal temperature tends to be 23-24 Celsius for growth and activity. (Cold water traps more oxygen)

Just like people, a fish's rate of metabolism slows then as they age, so it's best to 'remove' them once they get older.

2. Input Ratios,

As a general rule, you're gonna wanna feed the fish approximately:
40g fish food / square meter of grow space / day.

The Feed Conversion Ratio (FCR) is the ratio of the mass of food given to fish, to the amount of weight they gain. This is a statistic most important for people who specifically want to grow the fish.

It is important to note how this refers to the dry weight of the food, but the wet weight (I.E. not dehydrated) of the fish.

The Feed Efficiency Ratio (FER) is 1/FCR.

Using the general rule above, assuming we have 100 squared meters of grow space, we would be feeding the fish 0.4 kg of fish food per day;

(0.04 kg feed x 100 square meters of grow space) = 4.0 kg / day

So;

4 kg x 365 days = 1,460 kg fish feed per year,

1460 kg x FER = fish weight increase per year,

3. Life-Cycles.

These are the stages of a fish growing;

It's best not to introduce the baby fish to bigger ones until they are at least fingerlings, to make sure they don't get eaten.

It is best to stagger when you start growing groups of fish, so they are never all too old or too young.

4. Materials / Structures

Material must be Inert. so it isn't releasing chemicals into the water. Do not use copper or zinc. it is toxic to fish.

All water should be natural water. No tap water, due to treatments like chlorine, they kill bacteria. Also, not all natural water is the same, for example, well water may have the wrong pH.

You should also always have more water handy. Evaporation happens, it must be replaced somehow.

Fish tanks should ideally be 3 : 1; diameter : depth. - This is best for collecting sediment in the center

5. Feeding

It's best to have a formulated feed, so you know what is going into the system. Whatever you put in to the system will have on the balances of different nutrients.

6. Europhication from Algae

An algal bloom can lead to destruction of the environment needed for the system. Sunlight leads to algal growth.
